Blue border on a cell
A blue border marks a cell that was updated via the Updates Log — it also shows the date and time the update was logged. It does not mean there is a problem. Use it to quickly identify what changed and when.
Red cell highlight
A red cell flags a gap or issue that requires attention — a quantity shortfall, a rejection rate above threshold, or stock falling below the reorder point. Red does not update automatically on its own: it is triggered by data entered through the Updates Log or by formula conditions in the Inventory Planning sheet.
Treatment Status
Every update in the Updates Log has a Treatment Status column. Open = issue logged but not yet resolved. Closed = issue has been followed up and resolved. Keep this column up to date so the team knows what still requires action.

Place new PO when Total Available approaches Reorder Point. Check Master Data for supplier lead times.
Action trigger
Total Available
Current Stock + In Transit per destination. Includes in-transit stock regardless of expected delivery date.
Note: For time-sensitive decisions, always cross-reference Expected Delivery Date in the Inbound Tracker.
MOH — Months of Inventory
Total Available ÷ Monthly Sales Rate. Shows how many months of stock remain.
Action trigger: When Total Available reaches or falls below the Reorder Point, the cell turns red — a new PO must be placed immediately, or stock will run out before the next shipment arrives.

Thresholds & Rules — what triggers an alert
Rejection Rate > 2%
If Rejected Qty exceeds 2% of Received Qty, the cell turns red and Severity is classified as Critical. A quality issue requiring immediate follow-up with the supplier.
Container Utilization < 60%
Applies to international shipments only. Below 60% triggers a "Consider consolidation" flag in the PO Header. Domestic suppliers are not subject to this threshold.
Reorder Status: Reorder Now
Triggered when Total Available ≤ Reorder Point. Cell turns red automatically. A new PO must be placed immediately — stock will be depleted before the next shipment arrives.
Quick Reference — Where to go for what
Golden rule: When in doubt — start with Master Data for supplier info, Updates Log for any change, and the Dashboard for the current status of open issues. Never edit the Inbound Tracker directly.
